Introduction

Non-compliance with antitrust laws can be very damaging to a business. Its reputation can be hurt, its commercial strategies can be disrupted and dealing with the consequences of non-compliance can be very costly in terms of management time and potential fines being imposed. Companies operating in Europe are increasingly at risk of being investigated by ever more vigilant competition authorities armed with substantial investigatory powers. In a number of European jurisdictions, criminal penalties may also apply in addition to administrative fines. In recent years, record fines have been imposed by the European Commission on companies of all sizes in a number of sectors. The German Federal Cartel Office has also raised its fines and investigated a rising number of industries.

Competition authorities may carry out unannounced inspections of company premises (Dawn Raids), acting on external or internal information, to verify concerns that competition has been distorted.

In order to protect your company from antitrust non-compliance risks, the Allen & Overy mobile Dawn Raid Guideline

  1. provides suggestions on how to prepare for inspections by the competition authorities, and
  2. familiarizes you with the typical course of a Dawn Raid, the powers the inspecting officials have, the extent to which the company will have to cooperate and - most importantly – with steps to take to protect the company’s rights of defence.

All such inspections are governed by the procedural rules of the relevant investigating authority, so that different rules will apply depending on which authority inspects, in the case of Germany whether the European Commission or the local authority (Bundeskartellamt) investigates.

Additionally, the Allen & Overy mobile Dawn Raid Guideline forms part of a series of information on how to prepare for inspections by the competition authorities in those countries in which your company does business.
